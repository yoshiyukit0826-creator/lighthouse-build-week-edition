## Recommended paragraph

### Evidence from real-world operation

Lighthouse grew from a navigation system used in daily field operations before Build Week. A retrospective analysis of its pre-existing Google Sheets action log from March 8 through May 26, 2026 found that recorded negative operational events per active logging day decreased from 10.78 in March to 2.71 in May, a 74.8% reduction. The share of low-load days increased from 8.7% to 64.3%, while recovery-event logging remained essentially stable from April to May. These figures do not directly measure production output or establish causality, but they provide longitudinal evidence of fewer recorded disruptions, search events, and repeated mistakes as the operator and navigation system evolved together.

## Shorter version

A retrospective analysis of real-world action logs from March through May 2026 found a 74.8% reduction in recorded negative operational events per active logging day, while low-load days increased from 8.7% to 64.3%. This is not a direct productivity claim, but it is longitudinal evidence that operational friction decreased as Lighthouse and its operator evolved together.

## Wording guardrails

Use:

- “recorded negative operational events decreased”
- “longitudinal evidence”
- “consistent with fewer disruptions”
- “does not directly measure production output”
- “does not establish causality”

Do not use:

- “productivity increased by 74.8%”
- “Lighthouse caused a 74.8% improvement”
- “scientifically proven”
- “validated across workers”
